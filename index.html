Coach's note — make light weight build muscle

A 25 lb bell and 10 lb dumbbells are light for 190 lbs, so growth comes from effort, not load. Lighter weights taken close to failure build muscle as well as heavy ones — but only if you earn it. Three rules carry this whole program:

• Take the last set of every move to true failure (on safe lifts — push-ups, squats, rows, curls). On overhead and pressing work, leave 1 rep for safety.
• Slow the lowering to ~3 seconds and add the pauses/1.5-reps the app flags — that's how you make 25 lbs feel like 50.
• Keep rest short, 30–45 sec. Density is your friend in a 12-minute window.

Progressive overload (no heavier weights needed)

Double progression: hit the top of the rep range on all sets → then make it harder (slower tempo, add a pause, harder variation, or +1 set) → only buy a heavier bell once you've maxed those. The reps you log here are the scoreboard. If the bars on the Progress tab trend up over weeks, you're growing.

Nutrition — the part that actually builds it

Training is the signal; food is the material. To add muscle at 47 you need roughly 0.7–1 g of protein per pound of bodyweight — for you that's about 130–160 g a day, spread across meals (~30–40 g each). Eat at maintenance or a slight surplus; you can't build much on a deficit. Sleep 7–9 hours — that's when the repair happens. Creatine and the workout do nothing without these two.

Creatine — your Target tub

How much: 3–5 g (about a rounded teaspoon) of creatine monohydrate in any drink. When: timing barely matters once muscles are saturated — the only rule that counts is every single day, rest days included. Training at night, take it with dinner or right after the session.

No loading phase needed — a steady 5 g saturates muscle in 3–4 weeks with less bloat. Expect 2–4 lbs of water weight early; that's normal and expected. Stay hydrated. Check with a doctor first if you have kidney issues.

The 4-day rotation

A → B → C → D → repeat. The app auto-advances each time you log; tap any day to override. This hits every muscle ~2×/week — the proven sweet spot for growth and recovery. Aim for 4–6 sessions a week; take a rest day whenever you feel beat up. The streak survives planned rest.

Educational tool, not medical advice. Build form before intensity; stop on any sharp or joint pain.
